People Watching

Humans are among the most complex creatures in the animal kingdom. If animals could observe us, they might think we’re crazy. We walk on two feet, eat a wide variety of foods, experiment on and consume plants, and ride in metal machines, among many other things.

This often puts us on the opposite end of the spectrum from how most animals live. Take cats, for example. Their daily routine is usually simple: eat, sleep, run around, and occasionally seek attention. They might nap for half the day, only to wake up, meow for food or affection, and then go back to resting.

Their lives are relatively consistent and straightforward. In contrast, human lives can be unpredictable and varied—even from the moment we wake up. Our routines can change so much that we have apps to help manage our time. However, like cats, we also crave attention. We engage in activities such as playing video games, hanging out with friends, posting on social media, or even skydiving. (Okay, that last one is a bit extreme, but you get the idea.)

The ways we seek attention are far more diverse and complex than the way animals do. Humans are amazing creatures capable of displaying a wide range of emotions, thoughts, and behaviors. Our uniqueness comes from our individual experiences, upbringings, cultures, and genetics. So, embrace your differences, and remember that everyone around you is unique too.
