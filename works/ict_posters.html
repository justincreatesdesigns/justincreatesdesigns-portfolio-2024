ICT Posters

Posters I created to communicate with students at work.

Poster

Graphic Design

Overview

Working as an IT professional in a secondary school environment, we frequently encounter various issues ranging from hardware malfunctions to software glitches, or even unforeseen challenges. These posters are designed to address some of these minor yet persistent problems.

Observation

In my role, I often interact directly with students and encounter several recurring issues on a daily basis. Through close observation, I identified common problems that could potentially be solved through effective design.

Common things are:

  • Students attempting to force open locked doors, unaware of the proper procedure.
  • SD cards submitted without any identifying information.
  • Long queues at the office due to password forgetfulness.
  • Persistent knocking on doors despite the presence of a doorbell.

The Solution

After numerous attempts to address these issues verbally with little success, I decided to leverage my design skills to create visually appealing solutions.

Students are naturally drawn to attractive visuals and can easily understand messages conveyed through effective graphics and messaging.

Limitations

Initially, I considered the constraints I faced: limited time due to work demands, an oversized printer (EPSON P6000) unsuitable for small prints, and a colored printer located far from my office, making quick reprints inconvenient.

Objectives

  1. Be printed in black and white for simplicity and accessibility.
  2. Utilize A4 paper, readily available throughout the office, instead of specialized poster paper rolls and printer ink which is expensive to replace.
  3. Feature visually appealing designs that effectively communicate the intended message to students.
  4. Maintain a consistent visual identity for easy recognition and repetition.
