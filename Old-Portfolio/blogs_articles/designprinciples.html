Design Principles

Learn some of the basic principles of graphic design!

Digital media has been the norm in everyone's lives at this point, due to the huge amount of online platforms accessible to everyone. Conveying messages, ideas, stories, or products through proper communication has become increasingly noticeable. This has given rise to the concept of UX (user experience) by crafting a visual journey for the viewer using images, typography, colour, texture and many more. But where do we begin?

#1 Alignment

What do you see when you look at this photo?

Incase you didn't see it, it was the pack of beers that was misaligned! See how such a small detail can be so noticeable? Proper alignment helps ensure that you have a clean structure which will build a visual appeal to the viewer.

#2 Contrast

Contrast is a great way to catch attention by using unique elements that stand out. The best use of this is 2003’s Apple’s advertisement called the “iPod Shuffle.”

#3 Repetition

Repeating visual elements promotes consistency and unity which can also help in creating consistency in your design.

#4 Proximity

Proximity is the final principle to tie this all together. Grouping things together in your design allows viewers to collerate things in groups or individuals. Such as for example your navigation bars on phones or websites to content where like the photo above is accompanying text right now that allows you to participate in this activity!

That’s about it! Now that you're equipped with these 4 principles, get out there and bring your ideas to life.
