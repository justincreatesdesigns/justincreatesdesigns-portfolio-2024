Design Principles

Learn some of the basic principles of graphic design!

Digital media has been the norm in everyone's lives at this point, due to the huge amount of online platforms accessible to everyone. Conveying messages, ideas, stories, or products through graphic design has become increasingly noticeable. This has given rise to the concept of UX (user experience) by crafting a visual journey for the viewer using images, typography, colour, texture and many more. But where do we begin?

#1 Alignment

What do you see when you look at this photo?

Yes! The pack of beers that are misaligned. See how such a small detail can be so noticeable? Proper alignment helps ensure that you have a clean structure which will build a visual appeal to the viewer.

#2 Contrast

Contrast is a great way to catch attention by using unique elements that stand out. The best use of this is 2003’s Apple’s advertisement called the “iPod Shuffle.”

#3 Repetition

Take a look at McDonald’s for example. Take note of their use of colour, shapes and images. Do you realise there is a consistent theme? Repeating visual elements promotes consistency and unity which can also help developing an identity.

#4 Proximity

Again, just like how we started, what do you see? Yes! The french fries package. Proximity helps with categorising your visual elements in your graphic. Bringing elements close, far or around using this principle can display your visuals as a unit or in different sections.

That’s about it! Now that you're equipped with these 4 principles, get out there and bring your ideas to life.
